THE UTAHRAPTOR

  • Genus: Utahraptor
  • Species: Utahraptor ostrommaysorum, meaning "Utah's predator" (a single species)
  • Family: Dromaeosauridae (theropod dinosaurs commonly known as "raptors")
  • Suborder: Theropoda
  • Order: Saurischia
  • Subclass: Avetheropoda
  • Class: Sauropsida
  • Subphylum: Vertebrata
  • Phylum: Chordata
  • Kingdom: Animalia

classification & distribution

The Utahraptor lived during the Early Cretaceous period, specifically around 135 to 130 million years ago. It inhabited current day United States, specifically in the region of Utah. This is known because Fossils of Utahraptor have been found in the Cedar Mountain Formation, Utah. The Cedar Mountain Formation is known for its diverse ecosystem and paleo-findings which included dinosaurs such as ankylosaurs and iguanodonts species. It's important to understand that the distribution of Utahraptor during its time was limited to the areas where its fossils have been discovered. Since Utahraptor's fossil record is incomplete, it is reasonable to believe that there may have been other regions where it lived but have not yet been discovered or studied.

behaviors & adaptations

The Utahraptor probably died out because of its ever-so changing environment; the Earth cooling down and Utahraptors incapable of adapting to such changes. This might've been the main reason why Utahraptors everywhere ceased to exist after a time. They went to exist for around 5 million years before going extinct. Utahraptors were a warm-blooded large, feathered, actively predatory theropod. Utahraptor packs may have probably lived in forests or deserts/prairies where hunting wouldn't be a problem. (It's important to note, it is a big if when it comes to being cold or warm blooded, we simply don't have enough information to confirm.)

special features & identification

It was quite easy to identify a Utahraptor. Its body's significantly large than the famous velociraptor. They were sleek, feathered, bipedal, with a heavily built, ground dwelling body. The Utahraptor has a narrow, sharply defined snout and forward looking eyes. They may have had also small, serrated teeth that lined its jaws, which were used to dispatch its prey. The massive raptor also had a large "killing claw" on its second toe, which it used to capture and kill its prey.

fossils, discoveries, & size

who discovered the infamous killer?

This is an easy answer, Jim Jensen in 1975. However, his initial findings were too fragmented to identify the dinosaur accurately. It wasn't until 1991 where the Utahraptor was fully uncovered by James Kirkland, Robert Gaston, and Donald Burge during a dig sponsored by the Prehistoric Museum. All of the discoveries were in Utah, hence the name Utah-raptor. Even until today, we are still making great findings in this dinosaur’s ancient history. Games like Jurassic World: The Game and Path of Titans include this great dinosaur for players to find. It can be a fun and interactive way to see how this dinosaur may have been size-compared.

predators & prey

It is truly a debate, whether or not the Utahraptor hunted in packs, as it was so large and fierce by itself. There are many sources and research scientists where they believe Utahraptors did hunt in packs. It was although, believed strongly that they followed a typical Dino-family system depicted in movies, such as defending eggs or bringing back food to the nest. The Utahraptor was its most dominant carnivore in its ecosystem, eating pretty much any dinosaur it desired. Scientists believe that the diet of Utahraptor also included Iguanodonts, a large herbivore.

what fossil history are we talking about?

James Kirkland, the state of Utah's paleontologist, uncovered and named the Utahraptor in 1993. The fierce creature also became the official state dinosaur in 2018. He believes that Utahraptors at the site of Stikes Quarry got stuck in sinking pits of quicksand when they tried attacking plant-eating dinosaurs that were already trapped there. Their death and submersion actually were a long-term preservation of their body.

latest fossil discoveries

Very recently, Kirkland and a small team had removed a giant 18,000 pound block of Utahraptor fossils from Stikes Quarry. His new study includes an edit of the dinosaur's age, being 135 million years old, around 10 million more than previously theorized.

fossils, discoveries, & size

about breeding and life cycle

In fact, Utahraptors likely did not live a special life cycle compared to other dinosaurs. As many other dinosaurs, a Utahraptor probably started off in an egg and went through an incubation process. Its lifespan is unclear, however, many sources will say 70-80 years, whilst others may say a bit over 20. As expected, there are not any known special breeding behaviors. However, a recent find in that mega block as stated earlier includes the remains of six Utahraptor. It was the remains of a 16-foot long adult, four youngsters, and a possible baby 1-foot long Utahraptor. And as we may know, the mega block’s reason to why the bodies were so perfectly preserved in a time span of millions of years were because it was, meaning it could just be a coincidence. Kirkland believes otherwise, and that it was definitely a Utahraptor family. This is especially important because this could be the beginning of understanding Utahraptor behavior in an unexpected way.

size range & ways our dinosaur communicated

The Utahraptor was a pretty large dinosaur, weighing over half a ton to a bit over a ton, being 6 feet in height and up to 23 feet in length, Being significantly larger than the famous velociraptor, it was also probably the smartest dinosaur of all time. Dromaeosaurs, in fact, were one of the most intelligent group of dinosaurs ever to exist. This is because paleontologists figure out relative dinosaur intelligence based on the animal's Encephalization Quotient, or EQ. The EQ of troodontids is about 6 and dromaesaurs aren't far behind with an EQ of 5.8. For a more relative comparison, humans have the highest EQ of 7.4-7.8.

other utahraptor species & relative habitatual behaviors

What’s so interesting about our amazing creature is that we have only ever discovered this dinosaur to be of one species. That species being Utahraptor ostrommaysorum meaning “Utah’s predator”. The Utahraptor took its habit in the plains of North America. Specifically floodplains and open woodlands with riverine forests. The Utahraptor probably wasn't very fast, so it likely used cover to lie and wait for prey. Utahraptor packs also likely lived in forests or deserts/prairies where hunting wouldn't be problematic. It is approximated that they also had a certain amount of territory, around 3 miles all around. Their territory would have been near a water source which could suggest Utahraptor ate fish as well.
